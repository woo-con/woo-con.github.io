Vendors

Whether they're selling art, handmade trinkets, figures, or comics, there's something for everyone in our vendor area!

An image from the 2015 Woo-Con vendor floor.

Each year, Woo-Con has had a Vendor's area, where congoers can buy unique nerd memorobilia, art, handmade trinkets, and even snacks! There is something for everyone.

Vendor List

We are fortunate enough to have a ton of talented vendors each year, and this year is no exception! We greatly enjoyed having everyone vend at this past year's convention, and we would like to thank all of our fantastic vendors for helping make Woo-Con 2017 the best Woo-Con yet!

For posterity's sake, check out our 2017 vendor page here if you would like an idea of the kind of vendors that we feature here at the con.

This list is subject to change at any point before the convention. Here are the vendors that have signed on so far. Stay tuned for updates!

Go Figure!

Operated by College of Wooster student Mel Kadish ('19), this shop features handmade figurines and jewelry. With tons of fandom options and styles, we figure you should check this shop out! (badum-tss)

MargoMundo

MargoMundo is a local artist in Wooster returning for her second year at Woo-Con. She specializes in traditional and digital art, and some of her favorite fandoms are Steven Universe, Gravity Falls, and Voltron: Legendary Defender. At Woo-Con, she'll be selling some buttons, stickers, magnets, and prints, such as the one featured here (image credit here). She will also have original work and custom commissions available as well. Check out her Facebook page here, her Instagram here, and her RedBubble here! (Bio credit to MargoMundo. Thanks, Margo!)

Forze.del.Male

Forze.del.Male is, like many of the artists here, from Wooster. Currently pursuing a PhD in plant science, Forze finds lots of inspiration from the natural world, including crystals, snow, bones, and plants (of course) and combines these elements with her art. She will be attending Woo-Con with her sister, MargoMundo. For more art, she can be found on Facebook, Instagram, and RedBubble. (Bio by Forze.del.Mal--thanks, Forze!)

JRL Studios

Josh Lanning is a big ol' geek who loves to draw and dress up like a lady. He hopes you'll enjoy at least one of these two things, as he'll be doing both at WooCon! Check out his Twitter here, his Instagram here, and his Tumblr here. (Bio by JRL Studios--Thank you so much!)

ScoatGoat

Bio by ScoatGoat (Thanks, ScoatGoat!):
Hi! My name is Scott Henley (aka Scoatgoat). This is my first time as a vendor at a con. I'm a freelance Illustrator and I do character design. I also work on different posters and T-shirt designs.
Tumblr: https://scoatgoat.tumblr.com Teepublic: https://www.teepublich.com/user/scoatgoat

ScoatGoat and Martianworks will be sharing a table, so be sure to check out these two awesome artists! ScoatGoat will also be selling homemade candy; yum!

Glare Beware

(Bio by Nicole at Glare Beware. Thanks, Nicole!) Glare Beware was created in 2014, with "The Adventures of Nekosan", an original webcomic, being published online in February, 2015. The first web comic series is "The Adventures of Nekosan;" a collection of tales of a Yokai on an inter-dimensional journey, and all that dared to follow.

Glare Beware is also home to short comics, doodles, stickers, bookmarks, postcards, commissions, and more; all of which are created by the artist, Nicole. Nicole is an artist that graduated from Baldwin-Wallace University in 2013 with her Bachelor's of the Arts in Art Education. She has an artistic background focused mostly in painting and drawing. Outside of comics, Nicole wrote and illustrated a series of children's storybooks for Chinese students learning English as a second language. To find out more, visit www.glarebeware.com!

Nicole is also the con chair of one of our partners, the convention Gojotekicon! For more information about that awesome con, check out our Partners & Acknowledgements page!

How do I become a Vendor?

Fill out a vendor form and send it in with payment to Katherine Randazzo, vendor head, before the closing date of April 2nd, 2018.
If you are interested, or have any questions, email wooconvendors@outlook.com. Here is our vendor form!

Anything I should know about vendors?

  • Vendors do NOT take COW Cards! (Sorry, COW students.)
  • All vendors take cash, some take card.
  • We are not responsible for any products vendors sell.
  • For inquiries about products / complaints, please contact the vendor.
  • Vendors are independent of The College of Wooster.
  • Our vendors love to chat with congoers, so don't be afraid to talk with them!
